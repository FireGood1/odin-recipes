Nihari by Izzah Cheema

Go Back to Home

Description: This is an easy Nihari (Pakistani Beef Stew) recipe that requires 1 pot and less than 30 minutes of initial prep time. This recipe includes stovetop & slow cooker instructions for tender beef shank (or stew) meat

Ingredients:

Nihari Ingredients:

  • Beef Shank
  • Oil
  • Ground Spices
  • Garnish
  • Brown Rice Flour
  • Star Anise Cinnamon Bay Leaf
  • Ghee
  • Salt
  • Nihari Masala
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ginger

Recipe for the Nihari Masala:

  • ½-1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder or paprika, for color
  • 1 small black cardamom pod
  • 1 small (1- inch) mace blade (or piece of mace)
  • ¾-inch peepli (long pepper)
  • ¼ tsp fennel seeds (saunf)
  • ¼ tsp garam masala powder
  • ¼ tsp ginger powder
  • ¼ tsp onion powder
  • ⅛ tsp caraway seeds (shah zeera)
  • ⅛ tsp nigella seeds (kalonji)
  • pinch cane sugar

Note: To make this Nihari Masala powder, combine all of these ingredients in a spice grinder and process until a powder forms.

Steps:

  1. Step 1: Brown the onions & meat. Deeply brown onions give flavor, body, and depth to Nihari. Like with most stews, searing the meat so that it browns is essential, but don’t sear for too long as it can lead to tough meat.
  2. Step 2: Bloom Spices. Add spices and allow them to cook in the hot oil. If you’re worried about them burning, you can always add a splash of water.
  3. Step 3: Cook. For stovetop, allow it to come to a boil before lowering the heat to a simmer. For slow cooker, transfer the mixture to a slow cooker, add water and mix to combine.
  4. Step 4: Thicken. If you try to thicken the gravy by adding in flour, it will result in clumps. To avoid this, remove some of the nihari liquid (I always found using water dilutes the taste) and allow it to cool. Then stir it into the brown rice flour. Add this slurry to the nihari while stirring to prevent clumps. Allow this to cook and thicken.
